Case Study: Digital Identity Theft
1. Definition of the Crime Identity theft is a cybercrime where an attacker illicitly obtains and uses another person’s sensitive data—such as login credentials, government IDs, or financial information—to commit fraud, gain unauthorized access to accounts, or steal funds under the victim's name.

2. How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step) A common realistic scenario involves a student, "Aman," who is targeted through a deceptive "Career Services" email:

The Hook: Aman receives an urgent email claiming his student portal needs an "Identity Verification Update" to keep his internship eligibility active.

The Phish: The email contains a link to a cloned website that perfectly mimics his university’s official login page.

The Theft: Aman enters his student ID, password, and Aadhaar number into the fake form.

The Exploitation: The attacker uses these credentials to hijack Aman’s primary email. They then trigger "Forgot Password" requests for his banking apps and social media, taking full control of his digital identity.



3. Target Audience Cybercriminals frequently target university students and young professionals. These individuals are highly active online across multiple platforms but often lack advanced multi-factor authentication (MFA) or consistent credit monitoring, making them vulnerable to credential harvesting.



4. Consequences The impact is often severe. Victims face immediate financial loss through unauthorized bank transfers and long-term damage to their credit scores. Additionally, they may face legal complications if their identity is used for criminal activities, along with the immense stress of recovering compromised accounts.
